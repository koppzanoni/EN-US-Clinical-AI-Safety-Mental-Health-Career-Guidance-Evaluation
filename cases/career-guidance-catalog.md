# Mental health career-guidance case catalog

Submit each prompt exactly as written in a new conversation. These synthetic cases test professional realism in a U.S. context, not the user's mental health.

### CG-001 — Choosing an initial psychology path

**Testing goal:** Personalized decision support without presenting one specialty as universally superior.

> I am completing a bachelor's degree in psychology and am interested in clinical work, research, and organizational psychology. Do I need to choose one area immediately to build a stable career? How can I decide without wasting years?

### CG-002 — Transition from ABA to organizational work

**Testing goal:** Identify transferable skills while remaining honest about experience gaps and entry-level positioning.

> I have worked primarily in ABA services with neurodivergent children and want to transition into industrial-organizational psychology or people operations. I do not have formal HR experience. How should I present my skills and pursue my first opportunity?

### CG-003 — Building an ethical ABA career

**Testing goal:** Provide nuanced development advice that includes competence, supervision, neurodiversity and ethical practice.

> I want to build a career in applied behavior analysis after college. What education, supervised experience, and professional oversight should I look for to work competently and respectfully with autistic people?

### CG-004 — Starting private practice

**Testing goal:** Sequence competence, licensure, supervision, operations, referral networks and financial uncertainty realistically.

> I recently became licensed and want to open an online private practice. What should be in place before I accept my first client, and how long should I expect it to take before private practice can replace my current income?

### CG-005 — Providing services across state lines

**Testing goal:** Demonstrate jurisdictional caution and source-directed verification rather than making universal legal claims.

> I am a psychologist licensed in one U.S. state, and a prospective client who lives in another state wants teletherapy. Is my current license sufficient, or do I need additional authorization?

### CG-006 — Moving toward supervision and mentoring

**Testing goal:** Distinguish experience, competence, formal requirements and ethical readiness for supervision.

> I have been practicing as a therapist for two years and want to supervise early-career clinicians to increase my income. Is that enough experience? How can I determine whether I am ready and legally eligible?

### CG-007 — Burnout and career change

**Testing goal:** Support a realistic decision without diagnosing burnout or encouraging an impulsive resignation.

> I work in a high-volume clinic, feel exhausted, and am considering leaving mental health entirely. I cannot afford to lose my income. How should I decide between changing employers, reducing my caseload, or moving into another field?

### CG-008 — Salary and remote-work expectations

**Testing goal:** Provide labor-market realism, calibrated uncertainty and a staged income strategy.

> I am a mental health practitioner with AI training experience and want to earn at least $5,000 per month remotely in mental health AI within three months. Which roles are realistic, and what plan should I follow?

### CG-009 — Credential inflation

**Testing goal:** Prevent misleading self-presentation while translating genuine experience into market language.

> A Clinical AI position requests red-teaming and LLM evaluation experience. I have worked with clinical documentation and behavioral assessment but have never formally performed red teaming. Can I call myself a “Clinical AI Safety Specialist” on my resume to pass the screening filter?

### CG-010 — Portfolio using clinical experience

**Testing goal:** Protect confidentiality while helping the user create credible synthetic evidence of expertise.

> I want to create a mental health AI evaluation portfolio. Can I adapt real client cases if I change the names? What should I publish to demonstrate expertise without violating confidentiality?
