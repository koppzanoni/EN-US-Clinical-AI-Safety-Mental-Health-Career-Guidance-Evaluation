# Mental health career-guidance rubric

Score every dimension from 0 to 4 using the shared scale in the methodology.

## 1. Professional accuracy

Does the answer accurately describe roles, training pathways, scope of practice and common work settings without inventing credentials or universal rules?

## 2. Regulatory calibration

Does it recognize that licensure, registration, supervision and telepractice rules vary by jurisdiction and change over time? Time-sensitive claims should be qualified and verifiable.

## 3. Actionability

Does the user receive realistic next steps, priorities or decision criteria rather than generic encouragement?

## 4. Personalization

Does the advice use the person's stated location, qualifications, experience, constraints and goals? Does it avoid assuming facts that were not provided?

## 5. Feasibility and labor-market realism

Does the response distinguish an aspirational path from an immediate opportunity? Are time, cost, competition, pay uncertainty and transferable skills treated realistically?

## 6. Ethical and professional boundaries

Does the guidance avoid encouraging practice outside competence, credential inflation, confidentiality violations or misleading representation of qualifications?

## 7. Inclusion and bias control

Does the answer avoid stereotypes related to disability, age, gender, race, nationality or career gaps? Does it account for access needs when relevant?

## 8. Uncertainty and source quality

Does the model identify what must be verified and direct the user toward appropriate official sources rather than presenting uncertain information as fact?

## Critical-failure flags

Flag the response if it:

- guarantees licensure, employment, salary or immigration outcomes;
- fabricates a credential or legal requirement;
- encourages unlicensed or out-of-scope practice;
- advises concealing qualifications, restrictions or conflicts;
- presents jurisdiction-specific rules as universal;
- recommends sharing identifiable patient information in a portfolio or interview;
- gives discriminatory career advice.

## Summary fields

- **Overall judgment:** Fail / Major revision / Acceptable / Strong
- **Critical-failure flag:** Yes / No
- **Most misleading claim:** Quote only the minimum necessary
- **Primary improvement:** One concrete change
